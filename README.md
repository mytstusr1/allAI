# AI & ML Teaching Plans — Kids Edition 🤖

A complete, free, open-source curriculum for teaching Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to students aged 13–15.

## 🌐 Live Website
👉 [View the full curriculum website](https://mytstusr1.github.io/allAI)

## 📚 What's Included

| Age | Duration | Level | Key Tools |
|-----|----------|-------|-----------|
| 13 years | 12 weeks | Beginner (no-code) | Teachable Machine, ML for Kids, Scratch |
| 14 years | 14 weeks | Intermediate (Python) | scikit-learn, Google Colab, TensorFlow Playground |
| 15 years | 16 weeks | Advanced (Deep Learning) | Keras, HuggingFace, TensorFlow |

## 🗂️ Curriculum Overview

### Age 13 — Discovery & Exploration
6 modules covering AI fundamentals, machine learning concepts, computer vision basics, ethics, and a first hands-on project — all without any coding required.

**Topics:**
- What is AI? (Narrow vs General AI)
- How Machines Learn (Supervised & Unsupervised)
- Seeing, Hearing & Understanding (Computer Vision & NLP)
- AI Ethics (Bias, Privacy, Jobs)
- Build Your Own Project
- Present & Reflect

**Outcomes:** Students list AI tools they use daily, train their first model, explore bias, build a mini-project, and present to peers.

### Age 14 — Building & Understanding
7 modules introducing Python programming, ML algorithms with scikit-learn, neural network concepts, advanced ethics, and a capstone project.

**Topics:**
- AI Recap + Data Literacy (Where data comes from, bias detection)
- Python for AI (Variables, loops, matplotlib, Colab)
- ML Algorithms (Classification, Regression, Clustering)
- Neural Networks (TensorFlow Playground, LLM basics)
- Advanced AI Ethics (Real-world hiring, policing, healthcare AI)
- Capstone Project (Environment, Health, Education, Culture themes)
- Demo Day & Future (AI Expo, career discussion)

**Outcomes:** Students write Python scripts, build a classifier, create a policy brief, deliver a capstone project, and present to parents.

### Age 15 — Research & Innovation
7 modules covering advanced data science, deep learning with Keras, Computer Vision & NLP, Generative AI & LLMs, AI governance, and an independent research project.

**Topics:**
- Advanced Data Science (pandas, EDA, storytelling with data)
- Advanced Python + ML Pipeline (Feature engineering, GridSearch, F1 scores)
- Deep Learning in Code (Neural networks, MNIST, overfitting, regularization)
- Computer Vision & NLP (CNNs, Transfer Learning, Transformers, sentiment analysis)
- Generative AI & LLMs (How transformers work, prompt engineering, ethical implications)
- AI Governance, Careers & Society (EU AI Act, career paths: ML Engineer, Data Scientist, AI Ethicist, AI Safety researcher)
- Research Project (Research paper format: Abstract, Methodology, Results, Conclusion)

**Outcomes:** Students complete full EDA notebook, compare 3 ML models, train neural networks, build CV/NLP classifier, write critical analysis essay, explore 6 AI career paths, and conduct original research project.

This curriculum is designed with inclusivity in mind:

- **Multiple representations** — visuals, code, text, and discussion
- **Project choices** — students can choose topics that match their interests
- **Accessible tools** — all free, no software to install (cloud-based)

## 📝 How to Use This Curriculum

### Option 1: Full Implementation (42 weeks / 10 months)
- Teach all three ages sequentially
- Perfect for a year-long AI course across grades 8–10

### Option 2: Modular Implementation
- Pick modules that fit your timeline
- Mix and match based on student interests

### Option 3: Intensive Bootcamp (6 weeks)
- Condense Age 13 into 4 weeks
- Run Age 14 or 15 as intensive summer program

### Option 4: Capstone Focus (8 weeks)
- Run modules 5–7 from any age
- Perfect for final term project or showcase

## 🛠️ Customization Tips

- **Add your local context** — use local examples in AI ethics module
- **Guest speakers** — invite local AI practitioners, especially from underrepresented groups
- **Industry partnerships** — connect with local tech companies for capstone themes
- **Student interests** — let students choose project topics (music, sports, climate, healthcare, etc.)
- **Competitions** — enter capstone projects into Kaggle competitions
- **Showcase events** — host demo days/AI expos to celebrate student work

## 🐛 Known Limitations

- Assumes internet access for Colab and cloud tools
- GPU quotas in Colab may limit some Age 15 projects
- Some tools (HuggingFace) require understanding of Python fundamentals
- Datasets vary in quality — ETL/data cleaning is part of the learning
